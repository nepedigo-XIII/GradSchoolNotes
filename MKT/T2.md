# Marketing and Business Study Guide

## Product Line Breadth and Depth

**Breadth** refers to the number of different product lines a company
offers.\
**Depth** refers to the variety and number of products within each
line.\
Broad lines allow the firm to serve multiple market segments, while deep
lines allow the firm to satisfy customers with variations, upgrades, and
alternatives.

## Offering Life-Cycle

Products move through stages: **Introduction**, **Growth**,
**Maturity**, and **Decline**.\
Each stage affects marketing strategies, pricing, promotion, and
distribution.

## Purchase Process Model

The customer decision process includes:\
1. **Problem Recognition**\
2. **Information Search**\
3. **Evaluation of Alternatives**\
4. **Purchase Decision**\
5. **Post-Purchase Behavior**

## 6M Communication Model

A framework for planning marketing communication:\
1. **Market** -- Target audience\
2. **Mission** -- Communication objectives\
3. **Message** -- Content to convey\
4. **Media** -- Communication channels\
5. **Money** -- Budget\
6. **Measurement** -- Evaluation of results

## Push and Pull Model of Communication

**Push strategy:** Manufacturers push products through intermediaries
using promotions, trade deals, and incentives.\
**Pull strategy:** Firms promote directly to consumers to create demand
that pulls products through the channel.

## Media Mix Characteristics

-   **Firm-Controlled:** Company websites, brochures, packaging\
-   **Other-Controlled:** Independent reviews, news outlets\
-   **Customer-Controlled:** Social media posts, word of mouth\
-   **Owned:** Website, blog\
-   **Paid:** Ads, sponsorships\
-   **Earned:** Shares, mentions, organic buzz

## Intermediary Functions

Intermediaries perform tasks such as:\
- **Transactional:** Buying, selling\
- **Logistical:** Transporting, storing\
- **Facilitating:** Financing, advising

## Direct vs. Indirect Distribution

-   **Direct:** Producer sells directly to consumers (e.g., online
    store).\
-   **Indirect:** Use of intermediaries like wholesalers and retailers.

## Distribution Intensity

-   **Intensive:** Wide distribution for convenience goods\
-   **Selective:** Limited outlets for shopping goods\
-   **Exclusive:** Few outlets for specialty goods

## Dual Distribution & Multi-Channel Marketing

-   **Dual distribution:** Using two channels simultaneously (e.g.,
    direct + retail).\
-   **Multi-channel marketing:** Integrated use of multiple channels for
    convenience and reach.

## Channel Power and Conflict

**Channel power:** Ability to influence channel partners (reward,
coercive, informational).\
**Channel conflict:** Disagreements among channel members, horizontal or
vertical.

## Pricing Framework

Pricing includes:\
1. **Value Assessment**\
2. **Cost Analysis**\
3. **Competitive Positioning**\
4. **Pricing Objectives**\
5. **Tactical Setting**

## Price Elasticity

Measures how sensitive demand is to price changes.\
- **Elastic (\>1):** Demand responds strongly\
- **Inelastic (\<1):** Demand barely changes

## International Marketing Decisions

Includes market selection, entry mode decisions, product adaptation, and
global strategy formulation.

## Modes of Entry

-   **Exporting**\
-   **Licensing & Franchising**\
-   **Joint Ventures**\
-   **Foreign Direct Investment (FDI)**\
-   **Strategic Alliances**

## Adaptation vs. Standardization

-   **Adaptation:** Tailoring products/marketing to local markets\
-   **Standardization:** Using a uniform global strategy for efficiency
    and brand consistency
