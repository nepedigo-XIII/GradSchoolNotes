### *Raw Markdown Summary*

# 1. Introduction to Marketing Channels
A marketing channel consists of individuals and firms involved in making an offering available for use or consumption. Channels link firms to buyers and help implement strategy.

# 2. Impact on Marketing Mix
Channels influence segmentation, communication, pricing, offering, and go-to-market strategy.

# 3. Channel-Selection Decision
Start with the target market: who, where, when, how, what. Work backwards from buyer needs.

# 4. How Channels Add Value
Producers: promotion, logistics, ownership transfer, service.
Customers: information, convenience, variety, service, reverse channels.

# 5. Channel Length
Determined by number of intermediaries: distributors, retailers, brokers.

# 6. Direct vs Indirect Distribution
Indirect: fragmented markets, bundled offerings, low education needs, startups, powerful intermediaries.
Direct: differentiation, exclusivity, wide offerings, lack of capable intermediaries.

# 7. Target Market Coverage
Distribution density: intensive, selective, exclusive.

# 8. Buyer Requirement Satisfaction
Information, convenience & ambience, variety & complementarity, service.

# 9. Profitability
Depends on value creation, cost sharing, margins, power.

# 10. Trade Margins
m = RSP - MSP
MSP = RSP(1 - m)
RSP = MSP/(1 - m)

# 11. Intermediary Requirements
Support, discounts, fill-rate, lead-time, exclusivity, margins.

# 12. Multi-Channel & Dual Distribution
Multi-channel blends digital + traditional; risk of disintermediation.
Dual distribution uses 2+ channels for different segments or volumes.

# 13. Channel Conflict
Vertical: between levels.
Horizontal: same level.
Alignment required.

# 14. Decision Framework
Evaluate buyer needs, design coverage, manage intermediaries, resolve conflict.
